Fixed Prosthodontics

The Science of Fixed Restorations

At AZTech Dental Lab, our Fixed Prosthodontics department specializes in restoring oral function and aesthetics through permanent biological replacements. We combine classical dental mechanics with modern digital material science to deliver restorations that exceed clinical expectations.

Precision Crown Solutions

  • Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal (PFM): Combining the structural integrity of a custom-cast metal framework with the natural vibrancy of layered porcelain. Ideal for high-stress posterior environments.
  • Solid & Translucent Zirconia: Monolithic restorations designed for zero-prep or minimal-prep situations, offering superior fracture resistance and biocompatibility.
  • IPS e.max®: The gold standard for anterior aesthetics, providing lifelike translucency and light diffusion.

Advanced Bridge Frameworks

Our laboratory fabricates a variety of bridge designs, including Cantilever Bridges for unique support situations and Maryland Bonded Bridges for minimally invasive anterior tooth replacement. Each framework is digitally designed to ensure precise marginal fit and balanced occlusal forces.

Clinical Workflow & Coordination

  1. Digital Intake: Validation of intraoral scans or high-precision stone models.
  2. CAD Design: Virtual contouring of anatomical tooth forms and connector geometries.
  3. Materials Sourcing: Selection of medical-grade titanium or high-translucency zirconia.
  4. Final Artistry: Hand-finishing and characterization by our expert technicians.

Custom Abutments & Implant Integration

We leverage CAD/CAM processes to fabricate custom abutments that provide optimal emergence profiles for soft-tissue health. Whether using titanium or aesthetic zirconia, our secondary fixed components are engineered for long-term implant success.

Fixed Preparation Metrics

Restoration Type Marginal Prep Occlusal Reduction
PFM Crowns 1.0 - 1.2 mm 1.5 - 2.0 mm
Zirconia (Solid) 0.5 - 0.7 mm 0.8 - 1.0 mm
Lithium Disilicate 0.8 - 1.0 mm 1.0 - 1.5 mm

Technical Advisory

For long-span bridges, we recommend a minimum connector height of 3.5mm to ensure structural stability under functional loads. Contact our technical team for specific case consultations.
